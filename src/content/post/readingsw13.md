### Readings for Tuesday, 14 April

The topic for Tuesday 14 April will be detecting AI-generated content. The required reading is:

- Hanlin Zhang, Benjamin L. Edelman, Danilo Francati, Daniele Venturi, Giuseppe Ateniese, Boaz Barak. [_Watermarks in the Sand: Impossibility of Strong Watermarking for Generative Models_](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.04378). ICML 2024. [[PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.04378)] [[Website](https://hanlin-zhang.com/impossibility-watermarks/), which includes a short [Video Talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5DS2D8mLejg)] 

In addition to reading the paper, you should try the [Pangram AI Detector](https://www.pangram.com/) and read the blog post on [_How Pangram detects AI-generated content_](https://www.pangram.com/research/how-it-works). Your goal is to either generate or find human-written text that it misclassifies as AI generated (a _false positive_), or generate AI-generated text that it misclassifies as written by a human (a _false negative_). You can also try [GPTZero](https://gptzero.me/) (but it is too easy to find misclassifications for GPTZero for this to be a challenge).

Optional additional reading:

- Katherine Thai, Bradley Emi, Elyas Masrour, Mohit Iyyer. [_EditLens: Quantifying the Extent of AI Editing in Text_](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.03154). ICLR 2026. [[PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.03154)] 

- Bradley Emi. [_AI Conference Papers are Increasingly Being Written by AI: up 370% since 2023_](https://www.pangram.com/blog/academic-papers). 30 September 2024.

- Bradley Emi. [_Pangram Predicts 21% of ICLR Reviews are AI-Generated_](https://www.pangram.com/blog/pangram-predicts-21-of-iclr-reviews-are-ai-generated)]. 18 November 2025.
